Hi. I'm working on a bunch of articles about shaders for absolute beginners, with obnoxious amount of details and explanations. My goal is that (hopefully) you should become more familiar with HLSL and s&box shaders specifically. Although some things about shader still change, and some new stuff shows up sometimes, most of the material should stay up to date.

This is probably not very useful if you're planning to go very deep into graphics programming. I imagine that this stuff will be mainly useful for people like artists that want to make their stuff look even cooler in s&box.

Please keep in mind that this is a very early iteration of the entire website. There will be grammar and factual errors. There are probably code snippets that are dumb or weird. I'm still learning, too, and anybody is welcome to help & contribute.

Contributing

Anyone is welcome to contribute: article fixes, code improvements, article additions, and new courses/articles of your choice. Currently it's just me (wheatleymf) who's taking care of this website, but I'm absolutely open for anyone to help grow this thing into something more useful, readable and helpful.

This website is open for pull requests at https://github.com/wheatleymf/shaders/. This is where I ship all changes before they get deployed on this website.

Discussion

There's an ongoing thread in s&box discord for this website, if there's anything you'd like to ask or discuss with other people, this is probably the best way to do it: "Shaders for Noobs" discussion thread. This will require joining official s&box discord if you haven't joined it yet.
